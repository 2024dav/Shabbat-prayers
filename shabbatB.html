Blessing of the Wine

Genesis 2

1 Thus the heavens and the earth were finished, along with everything in them.
2 On the seventh day God was finished with his work which he had made, so he
rested on the seventh day from all his work which he had made.
3 God blessed the seventh day and separated it as holy; because on that day
God rested from all his work which He had created, so that it itself could produce.

Blessed art thou, King of the universe, who creates fruit of the vine.
Blessed art thou, King of the universe, who sanctifies us with His commandments,
and has been pleased with us.
You have lovingly and willingly given us your Holy Shabbat as an inheritance, in memory of creation.
The Shabbat is the first among our Holy days, and remembrance of our exodus from Egypt.
Indeed, you have chosen us and made us Holy among all peoples and have lovingly and willingly
given us your Holy Shabbat as an inheritance

Washing of Hands

Bark atah Adonai, Eloheinu melekh ha-Olam, asher kidushanu b'mtzvotov,
v'tzivanu ai netilat yadayim

Blessed art thou Oh Lord our God, King of the Universe, who has
sanctified us through your commandments and instructed us concerning the washing of hands.

Blessing for the Bread

Bark atah Adonai, Eloheinu melekh ha-Olam, hamotzi lechem mi ha'eretz.

Blessed art thou Oh Lord our God, King of the Universe, who brings
forth Bread from the Earth for our use even Yeshua The True Bread from Heaven.

Shabbat Shalom!

Grace After Meal

Birkat Ha-mon

Genesis 8:22

Blessed art thou Oh Lord our God, King of the Universe, who provides the fruit of the
earth for our use. We bless you for fulfilling continually your promises that while the
earth remains, seedtime and harvest shall not fail.
Teach us to remember that it is not by bread alone that we live, grant us evermore to feed
on Him who is the true bread from Heaven, even Yeshua our Messiah.
Amen
